* ## Ticket: Epic: Launch vehicle (Saturn V) {#1}

      status: open
      created: 2026-03-23 05:42:31 UTC
      updated: 2026-03-23 05:42:31 UTC

  Design, build, and test the three-stage launch vehicle to place 130,000 lbs into low Earth orbit.

  * ## Ticket: Task: S-IC first stage {#2}

        status: open
        created: 2026-03-23 05:42:31 UTC
        updated: 2026-03-23 05:42:31 UTC

    Five F-1 engines producing 7.5 million lbs of thrust. LOX/RP-1 propellant. Burn time 150 seconds. Structural design for max-Q loads. Separation system with retro rockets.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Structural design and propellant tanks {#77}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:54:44 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:54:44 UTC

      Design the S-IC airframe to withstand max-Q aerodynamic loads of approximately 735 psf while carrying 4.5 million lbs of LOX/RP-1 propellant. Tanks are constructed from 2219-T87 aluminum alloy with skin-stringer-frame architecture, welded in segments at the Michoud Assembly Facility.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Engine thrust structure {#78}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:54:44 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:54:44 UTC

      Cruciform thrust structure distributing 7.5 million lbs of thrust from five F-1 engines into the stage airframe. Must handle asymmetric thrust loads if one engine fails. Cross-beam and skin construction using 7075-T6 aluminum forgings.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Propellant feed and pressurization {#79}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:54:44 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:54:44 UTC

      LOX and RP-1 feed lines, prevalves, and helium pressurization system for tank ullage. The LOX suction lines are 17-inch diameter ducts routed from the forward tank through the intertank structure to each engine. RP-1 tank uses pressurized helium bottles immersed in the LOX tank.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Stage separation system {#80}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:54:44 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:54:44 UTC

      Eight solid-fuel retro rockets mounted in the S-IC/S-II interstage to decelerate the spent first stage after separation. Separation sequence involves firing explosive bolts on the tension ties, followed by ullage motor ignition on S-II and retro rocket firing on S-IC within a tightly sequenced 600-millisecond timeline.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Flight instrumentation and telemetry {#81}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:54:44 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:54:44 UTC

      Approximately 700 measurements covering thrust, pressure, temperature, vibration, and structural loads. Data multiplexed and transmitted via S-band telemetry during the 150-second burn. Instrumentation must survive extreme acoustic and vibration environments at liftoff.

  * ## Ticket: Task: F-1 engine development {#5}

        status: open
        created: 2026-03-23 05:42:31 UTC
        updated: 2026-03-23 05:42:31 UTC

    1.5 million lbs thrust per engine. Solve combustion instability through injector baffle design. Turbopump development. Test stand qualification at full duration.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Combustion instability resolution {#82}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:55:05 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:55:05 UTC

      Solve destructive combustion instability through redesigned injector face with copper baffles arranged in a radial pattern. Requires hundreds of bomb tests where small explosive charges are detonated inside the combustion chamber during firing to verify damping characteristics. This was the single hardest engineering problem on the F-1 program.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Turbopump assembly {#83}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:55:05 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:55:05 UTC

      Develop the turbopump producing 55,000 horsepower to deliver LOX at 24,811 gpm and RP-1 at 15,741 gpm. The gas generator drives the turbine at 5,500 RPM using LOX/RP-1 combustion products. Inducer design is critical to avoid cavitation at the pump inlet.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Thrust chamber and nozzle {#84}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:55:05 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:55:05 UTC

      Regeneratively-cooled thrust chamber with 178 tubes brazed together to form the chamber wall. RP-1 fuel flows down through the tube walls before entering the injector, absorbing heat to keep wall temperatures below 1,000 degrees F. Nozzle extension ratio of 10:1.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Gas generator and exhaust system {#85}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:55:05 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:55:05 UTC

      Gas generator producing fuel-rich combustion products at 1,200 degrees F to drive the turbopump turbine. Exhaust routed through a heat exchanger to warm helium for tank pressurization, then dumped overboard through the turbine exhaust manifold that forms the distinctive dark ring visible on the nozzle.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Component and full-duration test program {#86}

          status: open

      Static fire testing at MSFC Test Stand 4670 and Edwards Air Force Base. Full-duration 150-second firings to qualify engine for flight. Each flight engine must complete an acceptance test firing before delivery. Over 2,000 individual engine tests were conducted across the program.

        status: open
        created: 2026-03-23 05:42:31 UTC
        updated: 2026-03-23 05:42:31 UTC

    Heat shield for 25,000 mph reentry. Pressure vessel for 3-person crew. Forward hatch and docking mechanism. Reaction control system with 12 thrusters.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Heat shield design and fabrication {#87}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:55:10 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:55:10 UTC

      AVCO 5026-39 ablative heat shield bonded to a stainless steel honeycomb substructure. Must withstand temperatures exceeding 5,000 degrees F during reentry at 25,000 mph. The ablator is applied in a phenolic epoxy resin filled with silica fibers, hand-packed into honeycomb cells, then cured and machined to final contour.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Pressure vessel and crew cabin {#88}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:55:10 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:55:10 UTC

      Inner structure forming the pressurized crew compartment for three astronauts. Aluminum honeycomb sandwich construction with inner and outer face sheets. Must maintain 5 psi cabin pressure and withstand 12-G reentry loads. Crew couches mounted on energy-absorbing struts for impact attenuation.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Forward hatch and docking tunnel {#89}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:55:10 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:55:10 UTC

      32-inch diameter tunnel connecting CM to LM through the docking mechanism. Forward hatch must seal reliably after repeated docking/undocking cycles. The docking ring uses 12 automatic latches to secure the CSM-LM connection, with a manual backup.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Reaction control system {#90}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:55:10 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:55:10 UTC

      Two independent RCS rings, each with six 93-lb thrust engines using MMH/NTO hypergolic propellant. Provides three-axis attitude control during reentry and orbital maneuvers. The two redundant systems allow safe return even with one complete ring failure.

    * ## Ticket: Task: CM-SM separation and umbilical {#91}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:55:10 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:55:10 UTC

      Pyrotechnic tension-tie separation system with guillotine connectors for electrical and fluid umbilicals. CM must separate cleanly from SM prior to reentry. Three umbilical connections carry power, water, and oxygen from SM to CM during the mission; all must sever within milliseconds during separation.

  * ## Ticket: Task: Service Module propulsion {#10}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:55:39 UTC

Each leg has a 68-inch contact probe that triggers the "contact light" in the cabin when it touches the surface.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Descent stage structure and tanks {#99}

          status: open
          created: 2026-03-23 05:55:39 UTC
          updated: 2026-03-23 05:55:39 UTC

      Octagonal structure with four compartments for propellant tanks and one center compartment for the descent engine. Two sets of tanks arranged concentrically: fuel in the inner pair, oxidizer in the outer pair. Total propellant load is approximately 18,000 lbs.

    * ## Ticket: Task: Equipment storage and deployment {#100}

          status: open

Carries the ALSEP, sample return containers, tools, and on later missions the Lunar Roving Vehicle stowed in a folded configuration. The TV camera is initially mounted on MESA to cover the first EVA egress.
